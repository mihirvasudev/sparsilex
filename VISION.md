# SparsileX

## The Cursor of Research

SparsileX is an AI-native research and statistics platform. It does for data analysis what Cursor did for programming — takes a proven domain, makes it radically easier with AI, and ships it in a modern interface that works everywhere.

---

## The Problem

Research and data analysis is broken.

- **SPSS** costs $100/month, looks like it was built in 2003, and requires clicking through 15 menus to run a t-test.
- **Stata** is powerful but commands like `regress y x1 x2, vce(cluster id)` are hostile to anyone who isn't already fluent.
- **R** is free and infinitely flexible — and has a learning curve that kills 80% of would-be users before they get a result.
- **JASP** made it easier but it's still desktop-only, not AI-aware, and limited by its Qt/C++ architecture.
- **Excel** is where most of the world's "analysis" actually happens, and that's terrifying.

The core issue: the gap between "I have data and a question" and "I have a rigorous, reproducible answer" is enormous. It requires choosing the right test, checking assumptions, handling missing data, interpreting output, creating visualizations, and writing it up. Every step is a potential failure point.

AI closes that gap.

---

## The Vision

**You paste your data. You describe your research question in plain English. SparsileX does the rest.**

- Understands your data structure, types, and distributions automatically
- Suggests and runs the right statistical analyses
- Checks assumptions and flags violations
- Handles missing data, outliers, and transformations
- Produces publication-ready tables, figures, and APA-formatted results
- Generates reproducible analysis code (R or Python) for every step
- Explains what it did and why in plain language

It's not a chatbot bolted onto a stats package. It's a research environment built from the ground up around AI agents that understand statistics deeply.

## Core Beliefs

1. **The right analysis should be one sentence away.** If you can describe your question, you should get an answer. Not a manual. Not a Stack Overflow thread. An answer.

2. **AI must be transparent, not magic.** Every AI-suggested analysis comes with the reasoning, assumptions checked, code generated, and alternatives considered. Researchers need to trust and verify, not blindly accept.

3. **Reproducibility is non-negotiable.** Every analysis produces exportable R/Python code. Every result is deterministic. Every step is logged. If a reviewer asks "how did you get this number," the answer is one click away.

4. **Statistics should work everywhere.** On your laptop. On your phone while reviewing results on the bus. In your browser during a meeting. Not just on the one machine with the license.

5. **Power and simplicity are not opposites.** A beginner should be able to run a t-test in 30 seconds. An expert should be able to fit a mixed-effects model with custom contrasts in the same tool.

## The Name

**SparsileX** — from "sparsile" (relating to scattered stars, a statistical term for observations in sparse distributions) + "X" (the unknown variable, the experiment, the exploration). A name that is distinctly statistical yet forward-looking.

## Long-term North Star

SparsileX becomes the default environment where research happens. Not just statistics — the entire research workflow:

1. **Literature review** — AI searches and synthesizes relevant papers
2. **Study design** — power analysis, sample size planning, pre-registration
3. **Data collection** — survey builder, API integrations, lab instrument connections
4. **Data cleaning** — automated with full audit trail
5. **Analysis** — AI-guided with full transparency
6. **Visualization** — publication-quality, interactive, exportable
7. **Writing** — AI-assisted methods and results sections

One platform. From question to publication.
